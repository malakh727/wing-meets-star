A star does not guide you. It sits there, indifferent, burning.

You guide yourself *using* the star. The star is fixed. You are the thing that moves. The skill is not in the star — it is in the navigator's ability to translate a fixed point into meaningful direction.

This applies everywhere:

A piece of data does not tell you anything. You have to know what to ask it.

A framework does not solve your problem. You have to know how to apply it.

A compass points north. You have to know where north matters.

The tools are stars. The thinking is navigation.
