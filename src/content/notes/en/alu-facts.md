1. The Arithmetic Logic Unit is the heart of the central processing unit (CPU).

2. An ALU performs both arithmetic and logical operations because it contains two special units: an Arithmetic Unit (AU) and a Logic Unit (LU).

3. The small part responsible for arithmetic operations at the bit level is the **logic gate** — so the ALU consists of numerous logic gates connected in a complex way.

4. Mathematician **John von Neumann** first proposed the ALU concept in 1945.
