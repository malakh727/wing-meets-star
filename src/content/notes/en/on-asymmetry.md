Symmetry is the default. It is what happens when you center everything, make both sides match, balance the visual weight.

Symmetry signals *completeness* — the thing is done, resolved, finished.

Asymmetry signals *life* — something is in motion, something is unresolved, there is energy in the system.

The most interesting layouts have a dominant axis and a subordinate one. The eye follows the dominant path, then discovers the subordinate one. That discovery is the experience.

A 60/40 split is almost always more interesting than 50/50. The difference is small. The effect is significant.
