All electronic devices work using a fundamental element of computing called the **transistor**.

The transistor is the basic building block from which all microchips are built. It works in binary: if current passes through it, it stands for 1; if it doesn't, it becomes 0. From these 1s and 0s — called bits — a computer can produce any number.

## How do we represent other kinds of data?

Text assigns a number to each letter of the alphabet.

Images, videos, and all the graphics you see on a screen are made of tiny dots called **pixels**. Each pixel has a color, and each color can be represented with a number.

Sounds too — every sound is a series of vibrations in the air. Those vibrations can be represented as a waveform, and any point on that waveform can be represented by a number.

In conclusion, every operation in a computer — inputting, storing, processing, or outputting — is made of a series of numbers represented in binary, 0s and 1s, and the electrical signals in the circuits behind them.
