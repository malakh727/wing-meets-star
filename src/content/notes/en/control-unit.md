The Control Unit is an essential part of the central processing unit (CPU), responsible for directing the operation of the processor.

It tells the computer's memory, the ALU, and input/output devices how to respond to the instructions sent to the processor.

## Functions of the Control Unit

1. Coordinates the sequence of data movements into, out of, and between the processor's sub-units.
2. Interprets instructions.
3. Controls data flow inside the processor.
4. Receives external instructions and converts them into a sequence of control signals.
5. Controls execution units — the ALU, data buffers, and registers — within the CPU.
6. Handles multiple tasks: fetching instructions from memory, decoding them into commands, executing commands, and storing results in memory.
