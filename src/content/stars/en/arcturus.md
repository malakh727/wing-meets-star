He realized then that this fragile, trembling body had carried so much — so very much. Despite its desperate attempts to conceal the exhaustion, it finally announced itself: a passing illness destined to fade, and a heartbeat swinging like a pendulum…

Sometimes fast. Sometimes not.

He looked into her bewildered eyes and took her trembling hands in his, the way one holds a precious stone that glimmers…

"I will be your guardian."

That was the oath he made to himself. He did not want to fill her time with his presence, or let himself indulge in the sweetness of her words…

He wanted only to make certain she was always safe — in her place, doing what she wanted and loved, free to explore things on her own if she wished. He did not even want to be a guest. He wanted only to stand on the balcony and hear her laughter now and then. And her whispers when she spoke to the trees and the cats…

He believed his burning longing for her could be quenched by this alone — and he would never think, not for a single day, that he had brought more weight in her direction…

That he had burdened her, even beyond what she could bear — without meaning to. He did not want to enter that arena…

So in that moment he chose to be a guardian. A guardian of that generous sky, expecting nothing in return…

So he chose to stay — asking for nothing at all.
