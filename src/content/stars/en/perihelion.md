Perihelion: the point in an orbit where a body is closest to the sun.

It is not the peak. It is not the destination. It is a moment in a continuous curve — the single instant before the long drift away begins again.

Every comet has one. Every planet. Every object caught in a gravitational relationship passes through its perihelion and then begins the long withdrawal, the cold outward journey into the dark where the sun is just another star.

---

She thought about this on the flight home.

Relationships have perihelia too. Friendships. Collaborations. The years of a life when you are closest to some version of yourself that you will never fully inhabit again.

You don't know it's perihelion while you're in it. You only know afterward, looking back at the orbital path traced in memory, that there was a point of closest approach — and that you have been in the outward phase ever since.

---

But orbits are closed curves.

The body that drifts away is the same body that will return. The cold dark is part of the path, not the end of it. The distance accumulates and then reverses. The sun that seemed to recede was always the center.

Perihelion comes around again.

You just have to keep moving.
