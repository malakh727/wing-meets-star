Every atom in your body was forged in a star that no longer exists.

The carbon in your bones. The iron in your blood. The oxygen you are breathing right now. All of it was synthesized in stellar cores under pressures we cannot reproduce, at temperatures we can barely describe, in stars that burned for millions of years and then exploded outward across the void.

You are, in the most literal sense, made of old light.

---

There is a version of humility in this that has nothing to do with smallness.

We are not small in the sense of being insignificant. We are small in the sense of being *dense* — packed with the residue of ancient events. Every human being is a compressed history of the universe. A walking archive.

When we look up at the stars, we are not looking at something foreign. We are looking at our origin story, still in progress, still broadcasting.

---

The dust that made you is still out there, scattered and reforming. Some of it is already in other stars. Some of it is drifting in clouds that will not collapse for another billion years.

You are a brief local arrangement of very old material.

What you do with the arrangement is the question.
