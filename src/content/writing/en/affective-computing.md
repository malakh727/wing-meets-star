Many of us describe indifferent, insensitive people as machines. But have you ever thought about whether it is possible for machines to actually have feelings?

One of the most active fields in artificial intelligence today is **Affective Computing**.

Affective Computing is a field of study within artificial intelligence and cognitive computing, concerned with collecting data from faces, sounds, and body language to measure human feelings and emotions.

One of its most important practical goals is building a user interface between humans and computers that can determine a user's mental state and respond to it appropriately. One of the driving motivations behind this research is the ability to give machines emotional intelligence, alongside the simulation of emotion.

Machines must interpret a person's emotional state, adapt their behavior accordingly, and give an appropriate response to those feelings.

So think twice before describing insensitive people as machines — machines now have feelings.
