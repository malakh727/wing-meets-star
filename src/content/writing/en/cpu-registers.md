When you do a complex calculation, you sometimes need a pen and paper to track and keep numbers. A computer is no different — it needs a temporary location where the sources and results of arithmetic operations are stored.

That location is called a **Register**, an essential component of the CPU that works much like pen and paper.

Registers are a type of computer memory concerned with receiving, storing, and sending data and instructions — such as storage addresses in memory — used in real time by the CPU.
