You may have been stung painfully by bees and wished for them to disappear, and asked about the role of bees other than honey on your breakfast table. But a wish like the disappearance of bees is very dangerous — as if you wish the end of the world, because the fate of humans may be tied to them.

There is a saying attributed to Einstein: _"If the bees disappear from Earth, there will be only a few years left for humans to disappear after them."_

## What do bees actually do?

Bees are responsible for the production of approximately 70% of vegetables, fruits, seeds, and nuts through continuous pollination. If bees disappeared, entire food chains and networks would be at risk.

Our diet would be largely limited to corn, rice, and wheat. You would give up the idea of a morning coffee — bees are responsible for pollinating Arabica coffee plants. Cotton too, which would devastate the clothing sector.

Beyond food, the disappearance of plants caused by the extinction of bees would lead to climate disruption, increased global warming, and cascading environmental risks.

The main cause of the current bee decline is humans. Studies have indicated that pesticides affecting the nervous system of bees cause an imbalance in their colonies.
