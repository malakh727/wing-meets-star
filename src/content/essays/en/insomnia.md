## 3pm

Children are running in the playground. They crash into each other and laugh and keep going. You watch with a kind of helpless affection, knowing there is nothing to stop them — and that they will stop on their own, eventually, when every last drop of fuel has run out. They'll go home hoping for another day of it.

## 3am

You are lying in bed staring at a ceiling that is playing host to everything you were supposed to have sorted out by now.

Your thoughts are doing what the children were doing. They run, they collide, they multiply. One thought pulls in three more. You tell them to stop — you actually say it, somewhere in your chest — but they have the energy of things that don't know they're supposed to be tired.

There's no way to outrun a thought at 3am. You just have to let it go where it's going.

---

Eventually exhaustion wins the argument. Your eyes close not because you decided to sleep but because the machinery gave out. The thoughts get sealed inside and turn into dreams — vivid, too-real, the kind that make you reach for something when you wake up and find your hand empty.

Morning comes in pieces. A bird. Then more birds. Water on your face. Someone saying *good morning* in a voice that doesn't know what the night was like.

---

The day proceeds. You watch people move through it at full capacity — or what looks like full capacity — and then you watch that capacity quietly drain as the hours pass. By evening most faces have the same expression: the specific blankness of a person who has used themselves up.

The day ends. It always ends.

---

Last night's insomnia lit something in you that hasn't gone out yet. Every sense turned up too high. The dark reminds you of old sadness. The quiet reminds you of how often you are alone with yourself. The boredom doesn't remind you of anything — it *is* the thing, the actual texture of what you're inside.

And then the questions come, the ones that only arrive when you're too tired to fend them off:

*Why am I here?*

*Does it matter that I was?*

*Is there a difference between existing and performing existence?*

No answers at 3am. Just the questions, and the ceiling, and the distant sound of the world going on without waiting for you to figure it out.
